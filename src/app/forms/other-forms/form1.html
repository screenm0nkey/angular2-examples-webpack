Form 1 Basic form using NgForm and NgControl


        <form (ngSubmit)="onSubmit(f)" #f="ngForm">
            <input type="text" ngControl="sku">
        </form>
    
    NgForm does something handy but non-obvious: it includes the form tag in its
    selector (instead of requiring you to explicitly add ngForm as an attribute).
    What this means is that if you inject FORM_DIRECTIVES, NgForm will get
    automatically attached to any <form> tags you have in your view. This is really
    useful but potentially confusing because it happens behind the scenes.

    There are two important pieces of functionality that NgForm gives us:

        A ControlGroup named ngForm
        A (ngSubmit) output

    ngControl must be used as a child of NgForm (or NgFormModel in form2).
    Don't try to use ngControl without an NgForm parent or you'll have problems.

    FORM_DIRECTIVES gives us
        ngControl
        ngControlGroup
        ngForm
        ngFormModel

    If you inject FORM_DIRECTIVES, NgForm will get automatically attached
    to any FORM tags you have in your view.

    NgForm gives us:
        A ControlGroup named "ngForm"
        A (ngSubmit) output event. (ngSubmit) - comes from NgForm directive

    "#f" will be a local variable pointing to the ngForm "ControlGroup" object
